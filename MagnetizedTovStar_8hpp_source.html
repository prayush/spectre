26 /*!
27  * \brief Magnetized TOV star initial data, where metric terms only account for
28  * the hydrodynamics not the magnetic fields.
29  *
30  * Superposes a poloidal magnetic field on top of a TOV solution where the
31  * vector potential has the form
32  *
33  * \f{align*}{
34  * A_{\phi} = A_b \varpi^2 \max(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}}, 0)^{n_s}
35  * \f}
36  *
37  * where \f$A_b\f$ controls the amplitude of the magnetic field,
38  * \f$\varpi^2=x^2+y^2=r^2-z^2\f$ is the cylindrical radius,
39  * \f$n_s\f$ controls the degree of differentiability, and
40  * \f$p_{\mathrm{cut}}\f$ controls the pressure cutoff below which the magnetic
41  * field is zero.
42  *
43  * In Cartesian coordinates the vector potential is:
44  *
45  * \f{align*}{
46  * A_x&=-\frac{y}{\varpi^2}A_\phi, \\
47  * A_y&=\frac{x}{\varpi^2}A_\phi, \\
48  * A_z&=0,
49  * \f}
50  *
51  * For the poloidal field this means
52  *
53  * \f{align*}{
54  * A_x &= -y A_b\max(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}}, 0)^{n_s}, \\
55  * A_y &= x A_b\max(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}}, 0)^{n_s}.
56  * \f}
57  *
58  * The magnetic field is computed from the vector potential as
59  *
60  * \f{align*}{
61  * B^i&=n_a\epsilon^{aijk}\partial_jA_k \\
62  * &=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{\gamma}}[ijk]\partial_j A_k,
63  * \f}
64  *
65  * where \f$[ijk]\f$ is the total anti-symmetric symbol. This means that
66  *
67  * \f{align*}{
68  * B^x&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\gamma}} (\partial_z A_y-\partial_y A_z), \\
69  * B^y&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\gamma}} (\partial_x A_z - \partial_z A_x), \\
70  * B^z&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\gamma}} (\partial_y A_x - \partial_x A_y).
71  * \f}
72  *
73  * Focusing on the region where the field is non-zero we have:
74  *
75  * \f{align*}{
76  * \partial_x A_y
77  * &= A_b(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s}+
78  * \frac{x^2}{r}A_bn_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_r p \\
79  * \partial_y A_x
80  * &= -A_b(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s}-
81  * \frac{y^2}{r}A_bn_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_r p \\
82  * \partial_z A_x
83  * &= -\frac{yz}{r}A_bn_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_rp \\
84  * \partial_z A_y
85  * &= \frac{xz}{r}A_bn_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_rp
86  * \f}
87  *
88  * The magnetic field is given by:
89  *
90  * \f{align*}{
91  * B^x&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\gamma}}\frac{xz}{r}
92  * A_bn_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_rp \\
93  * B^y&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\gamma}}\frac{yz}{r}
94  * A_bn_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_rp \\
95  * B^z&=-\frac{A_b}{\sqrt{\gamma}}\left[
96  * 2(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s} \phantom{\frac{a}{b}}\right. \\
97  * &\left.+\frac{x^2+y^2}{r}
98  * n_s(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s-1}\partial_r p
99  * \right]
100  * \f}
101  *
102  * Taking the small-\f$r\f$ limit gives the \f$r=0\f$ magnetic field:
103  *
104  * \f{align*}{
105  * B^x&=0, \\
106  * B^y&=0, \\
107  * B^z&=-\frac{A_b}{\sqrt{\gamma}}
108  * 2(p-p_{\mathrm{cut}})^{n_s}.
109  * \f}
110  *
111  * While the amplitude \f$A_b\f$ is specified in the code, it is more natural
112  * to work with the magnetic field strength, which is given by \f$\sqrt{b^2}\f$
113  * (where \f$b^a\f$ is the comoving magnetic field), and in CGS units is
114  *
115  * \f{align*}{
116  * |B_{\mathrm{CGS}}|&= \sqrt{4 \pi b^2}
117  * \left(\frac{c^2}{G M_\odot}\right) \left(\frac{c}{\sqrt{4 \pi \epsilon_0
118  * G}}\right) \\
119  * &= \sqrt{b^2} \times 8.352\times10^{19}\mathrm{G} \,.
120  * \f}
121  *
122  * We now give values used for standard tests of magnetized stars.
123  * - \f$\rho_c(0)=1.28\times10^{-3}\f$
124  * - \f$K=100\f$
125  * - \f$\Gamma=2\f$
126  * - %Domain \f$[-20,20]^3\f$
127  * - Units \f$M=M_\odot\f$
128  * - A target final time 20ms means \f$20\times10^{-3}/(5\times10^{-6})=4000M\f$
129  * - The mass of the star is \f$1.4M_{\odot}\f$
130  *
131  * Parameters for desired magnetic field strength:
132  * - For \f$n_s=0\f$ and \f$p_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.04p_{\max}\f$ setting
133  * \f$A_b=6\times10^{-5}\f$ yields a maximum mganetic field strength of
134  * \f$1.002\times10^{16}G\f$.
135  * - For \f$n_s=1\f$ and \f$p_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.04p_{\max}\f$ setting
136  * \f$A_b=0.4\f$ yields a maximum mganetic field strength of
137  * \f$1.05\times10^{16}G\f$.
138  * - For \f$n_s=2\f$ and \f$p_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.04p_{\max}\f$ setting
139  * \f$A_b=2500\f$ yields a maximum mganetic field strength of
140  * \f$1.03\times10^{16}G\f$.
141  * - For \f$n_s=3\f$ and \f$p_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.04p_{\max}\f$ setting
142  * \f$A_b=1.65\times10^{7}\f$ yields a maximum mganetic field strength of
143  * \f$1.07\times10^{16}G\f$.
144  *
145  * Note that the magnetic field strength goes as \f$A_b\f$ so any desired value
146  * can be achieved by a linear scaling.
147  */
