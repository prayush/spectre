40 /*!
41  * \brief Functionality related to discontinuous Galerkin discretizations of
42  * elliptic equations
43  *
44  * The following is a brief overview of the elliptic DG schemes that are
45  * implemented here. A publication that describes the schemes in detail is in
46  * preparation and will be referenced here.
47  *
48  * The DG schemes apply to any elliptic PDE that can be formulated in
49  * first-order flux-form
50  *
51  * \f{equation}
52  * -\partial_i F^i_\alpha + S_\alpha = f_\alpha(x)
53  * \f}
54  *
55  * in terms of fluxes \f$F^i_\alpha\f$, sources \f$S_\alpha\f$ and fixed-sources
56  * \f$f_\alpha(x)\f$. The fluxes and sources are functionals of the "primal"
57  * system variables \f$u_A(x)\f$ and their corresponding "auxiliary" variables
58  * \f$v_A(x)\f$. The uppercase index enumerates the variables such that
59  * \f$v_A\f$ is the auxiliary variable corresponding to \f$u_A\f$. Greek indices
60  * enumerate _all_ variables. In documentation related to particular elliptic
61  * systems we generally use the canonical system-specific symbols for the fields
62  * in place of these indices. See the `Poisson::FirstOrderSystem` and the
63  * `Elasticity::FirstOrderSystem` for examples.
64  *
65  * The DG discretization of equations in this first-order form amounts to
66  * projecting the equations on the set of basis functions that we also use to
67  * represent the fields on the computational grid. The currently implemented DG
68  * operator uses Lagrange interpolating polynomials w.r.t.
69  * Legendre-Gauss-Lobatto collocation points as basis functions. Support for
70  * Legendre-Gauss collocation points can be added if needed. Skipping all
71  * further details here, the discretization results in a linear equation
72  * \f$A(u)=b\f$ over all grid points and primal variables. Solving the elliptic
73  * equations amounts to numerically inverting the DG operator \f$A\f$, typically
74  * without ever constructing the full matrix but by employing an iterative
75  * linear solver that repeatedly applies the DG operator to "test data". Note
76  * that the DG operator applies directly to the primal variables. Auxiliary
77  * variables are only computed temporarily and don't inflate the size of the
78  * operator. This means the DG operator essentially computes second derivatives
79  * of the primal variables, modified by the fluxes and sources of the system
80  * as well as by DG boundary corrections that couple grid points across element
81  * boundaries.
82  *
83  * \par Boundary corrections:
84  * In this implementation we employ the "internal penalty" DG scheme that
85  * couples grid points across nearest-neighbor elements through the fluxes:
86  *
87  * \f{align}
88  * (n_i F^i_v)^* &= \frac{1}{2} n_i \left(
89  * F^i_v(u^\mathrm{int}) +
90  * F^i_v(u^\mathrm{ext})\right) \\
91  * (n_i F^i_u)^* &= \frac{1}{2} n_i \left(
92  * F^i_u(\partial_j F^j_v(u^\mathrm{int}) - S_v(u^\mathrm{int})) +
93  * F^i_u(\partial_j F^j_v(u^\mathrm{ext}) - S_v(u^\mathrm{ext}))\right)
94  * - \sigma n_i \left(
95  * F^i_u(n_j F^j_v(u^\mathrm{int})) -
96  * F^i_u(n_j F^j_v(u^\mathrm{ext}))\right)
97  * \f}
98  *
99  * Note that \f$n_i\f$ denotes the face normal on the "interior" side of the
100  * element under consideration. We assume \f$n^\mathrm{ext}_i=-n_i\f$ in the
101  * implementation, i.e. face normals don't depend on the dynamic variables
102  * (which may be discontinuous on element faces). This is the case for the
103  * problems we are expecting to solve, because those will be on fixed background
104  * metrics (e.g. a conformal metric for the XCTS system). Numerically, the face
105  * normals on either side of a mortar may nonetheless be different because the
106  * two faces adjacent to the mortar may resolve them at different resolutions.
107  *
108  * Also note that the numerical fluxes intentionally don't depend on the
109  * auxiliary field values \f$v\f$. This property allows us to communicate data
110  * for both the primal and auxiliary boundary corrections together, instead of
111  * communicating them in two steps. If we were to resort to a two-step
112  * communication we could replace the divergence in \f$(n_i F^i_u)^*\f$ with
113  * \f$v\f$, which would result in a generalized "stabilized central flux" that
114  * is slightly less sparse than the internal penalty flux (see e.g.
115  * \cite HesthavenWarburton, section 7.2). We could also choose to ignore the
116  * fluxes in the penalty term, but preliminary tests suggest that this may hurt
117  * convergence.
118  *
119  * For a Poisson system (see `Poisson::FirstOrderSystem`) this numerical flux
120  * reduces to the standard internal penalty flux (see e.g.
121  * \cite HesthavenWarburton, section 7.2, or \cite Arnold2002):
122  *
123  * \f{align}
124  * (n_i F^i_{v_j})^* &= n_j u^* = \frac{1}{2} n_j \left(
125  * u^\mathrm{int} + u^\mathrm{ext}\right) \\
126  * (n_i F^i_u)^* &= n_i v_i^* = \frac{1}{2} n_i \left(
127  * \partial_i u^\mathrm{int} + \partial_i u^\mathrm{ext}\right)
128  * - \sigma \left(u^\mathrm{int} - u^\mathrm{ext}\right)
129  * \f}
130  *
131  * where a sum over repeated indices is assumed, since the equation is
132  * formulated on a Euclidean geometry.
133  *
134  * The penalty factor \f$\sigma\f$ is responsible for removing zero eigenmodes
135  * and impacts the conditioning of the linear operator to be solved. See
136  * `elliptic::dg::penalty` for details. For the element size that goes into
137  * computing the penalty we choose
138  * \f$h=\frac{J_\mathrm{volume}}{J_\mathrm{face}}\f$, i.e. the ratio of Jacobi
139  * determinants from logical to inertial coordinates in the element volume and
140  * on the element face, both evaluated on the face (see \cite Vincent2019qpd).
141  * Since both \f$N_\mathrm{points}\f$ and \f$h\f$ can be different on either
142  * side of the element boundary we take the maximum of \f$N_\mathrm{points}\f$
143  * and the pointwise minimum of \f$h\f$ across the element boundary as is done
144  * in \cite Vincent2019qpd. Note that we use the number of points
145  * \f$N_\mathrm{points}\f$ where \cite Vincent2019qpd uses the polynomial degree
146  * \f$N_\mathrm{points} - 1\f$ because we found unstable configurations on
147  * curved meshes when using the polynomial degree. Optimizing the penalty on
148  * curved meshes is subject to further investigation.
149  */

186 /// Construct `elliptic::dg::BoundaryData` assuming the variable data on the
187 /// element is zero, and project it to the mortar.
188 template <typename PrimalMortarFields, typename PrimalMortarFluxes, size_t Dim>
189 BoundaryData<PrimalMortarFields, PrimalMortarFluxes>
190 zero_boundary_data_on_mortar(
191  const Direction<Dim>& direction, const Mesh<Dim>& mesh,
192  const Scalar<DataVector>& face_normal_magnitude,
193  const Mesh<Dim - 1>& mortar_mesh,
194  const ::dg::MortarSize<Dim - 1>& mortar_size) noexcept {
195  const auto face_mesh = mesh.slice_away(direction.dimension());
196  const size_t face_num_points = face_mesh.number_of_grid_points();
197  BoundaryData<PrimalMortarFields, PrimalMortarFluxes> boundary_data{
198  face_num_points};
199  boundary_data.field_data.initialize(face_num_points, 0.);
200  get<Tags::PerpendicularNumPoints>(boundary_data.extra_data) =
201  mesh.extents(direction.dimension());
202  get(get<Tags::ElementSize>(boundary_data.field_data)) =
203  2. / get(face_normal_magnitude);
204  return Spectral::needs_projection(face_mesh, mortar_mesh, mortar_size)
205  ? boundary_data.project_to_mortar(face_mesh, mortar_mesh,
206  mortar_size)
207  : std::move(boundary_data);
208 }

257  template <bool DataIsZero, typename... PrimalVars, typename... AuxiliaryVars,
258  typename... PrimalFluxesVars, typename... AuxiliaryFluxesVars,
259  typename... PrimalMortarVars, typename... PrimalMortarFluxes,
260  typename TemporalId, typename ApplyBoundaryCondition,
261  typename... FluxesArgs, typename... SourcesArgs,
262  typename DirectionsPredicate = AllDirections>
263  static void prepare_mortar_data(
264  const gsl::not_null<Variables<tmpl::list<AuxiliaryVars...>>*>
265  auxiliary_vars,
266  const gsl::not_null<Variables<tmpl::list<AuxiliaryFluxesVars...>>*>
267  auxiliary_fluxes,
268  const gsl::not_null<Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalFluxesVars...>>*>
269  primal_fluxes,
270  const gsl::not_null<::dg::MortarMap<
271  Dim, MortarData<TemporalId, tmpl::list<PrimalMortarVars...>,
272  tmpl::list<PrimalMortarFluxes...>>>*>
273  all_mortar_data,
274  const Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalVars...>>& primal_vars,
275  const Element<Dim>& element, const Mesh<Dim>& mesh,
276  const InverseJacobian<DataVector, Dim, Frame::Logical, Frame::Inertial>&
277  inv_jacobian,
278  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, tnsr::i<DataVector, Dim>>&
279  internal_face_normals,
280  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, tnsr::i<DataVector, Dim>>&
281  external_face_normals,
282  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, Scalar<DataVector>>&
283  internal_face_normal_magnitudes,
284  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, Scalar<DataVector>>&
285  external_face_normal_magnitudes,
286  const ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, Mesh<Dim - 1>>& all_mortar_meshes,
287  const ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, ::dg::MortarSize<Dim - 1>>& all_mortar_sizes,
288  const TemporalId& temporal_id,
289  const ApplyBoundaryCondition& apply_boundary_condition,
290  const std::tuple<FluxesArgs...>& fluxes_args,
291  const std::tuple<SourcesArgs...>& sources_args,
292  const DirectionMap<Dim, std::tuple<FluxesArgs...>>&
293  fluxes_args_on_internal_faces,
294  const DirectionMap<Dim, std::tuple<FluxesArgs...>>&
295  fluxes_args_on_external_faces,
296  const DirectionsPredicate& directions_predicate =
297  AllDirections{}) noexcept {
298  static_assert(
299  sizeof...(PrimalVars) == sizeof...(PrimalFields) and
300  sizeof...(AuxiliaryVars) == sizeof...(AuxiliaryFields) and
301  sizeof...(PrimalFluxesVars) == sizeof...(PrimalFluxes) and
302  sizeof...(AuxiliaryFluxesVars) == sizeof...(AuxiliaryFluxes),
303  "The number of variables must match the number of system fields.");
304  static_assert(
305  (std::is_same_v<typename PrimalVars::type,
306  typename PrimalFields::type> and
307  ...) and
308  (std::is_same_v<typename AuxiliaryVars::type,
309  typename AuxiliaryFields::type> and
310  ...) and
311  (std::is_same_v<typename PrimalFluxesVars::type,
312  typename PrimalFluxes::type> and
313  ...) and
314  (std::is_same_v<typename AuxiliaryFluxesVars::type,
315  typename AuxiliaryFluxes::type> and
316  ...),
317  "The variables must have the same tensor types as the system fields.");
318 #ifdef SPECTRE_DEBUG
319  for (size_t d = 0; d < Dim; ++d) {
320  ASSERT(mesh.basis(d) == Spectral::Basis::Legendre and
321  mesh.quadrature(d) == Spectral::Quadrature::GaussLobatto,
322  "The elliptic DG operator is currently only implemented for "
323  "Legendre-Gauss-Lobatto grids. Found basis '"
324  << mesh.basis(d) << "' and quadrature '" << mesh.quadrature(d)
325  << "' in dimension " << d << ".");
326  }
327 #endif // SPECTRE_DEBUG
328  const size_t num_points = mesh.number_of_grid_points();
329 
330  // This function and the one below allocate various Variables to compute
331  // intermediate quantities. It could be a performance optimization to reduce
332  // the number of these allocations and/or move some of the memory buffers
333  // into the DataBox to keep them around permanently. The latter should be
334  // informed by profiling.
335 
336  // Compute the auxiliary variables, and from those the primal fluxes. The
337  // auxiliary variables are the variables `v` in the auxiliary equations
338  // `-div(F_v(u)) + S_v + v = 0` where `F_v` and `S_v` are the auxiliary
339  // fluxes and sources. From the auxiliary variables we compute the primal
340  // fluxes `F_u(v)` for the primal equation `-div(F_u(v)) + S_u = f(x)`. Note
341  // that before taking the second derivative, boundary corrections from the
342  // first derivative have to be added to `F_u(v)`. Therefore the second
343  // derivative is taken after a communication break in the `apply_operator`
344  // function below.
345  if constexpr (DataIsZero) {
346  (void)auxiliary_vars;
347  (void)auxiliary_fluxes;
348  primal_fluxes->initialize(num_points, 0.);
349  } else {
350  // Compute the auxiliary fluxes
351  auxiliary_fluxes->initialize(num_points);
352  std::apply(
353  [&auxiliary_fluxes,
354  &primal_vars](const auto&... expanded_fluxes_args) noexcept {
355  FluxesComputer::apply(
356  make_not_null(&get<AuxiliaryFluxesVars>(*auxiliary_fluxes))...,
357  expanded_fluxes_args..., get<PrimalVars>(primal_vars)...);
358  },
359  fluxes_args);
360  divergence(auxiliary_vars, *auxiliary_fluxes, mesh, inv_jacobian);
361  // Subtract the sources. Invert signs because the sources computers _add_
362  // the sources, i.e. they compute the `+ S_v` term defined above.
363  *auxiliary_vars *= -1.;
364  std::apply(
365  [&auxiliary_vars,
366  &primal_vars](const auto&... expanded_sources_args) noexcept {
367  SourcesComputer::apply(
368  make_not_null(&get<AuxiliaryVars>(*auxiliary_vars))...,
369  expanded_sources_args..., get<PrimalVars>(primal_vars)...);
370  },
371  sources_args);
372  *auxiliary_vars *= -1.;
373  // Compute the primal fluxes
374  primal_fluxes->initialize(num_points);
375  std::apply(
376  [&primal_fluxes,
377  &auxiliary_vars](const auto&... expanded_fluxes_args) noexcept {
378  FluxesComputer::apply(
379  make_not_null(&get<PrimalFluxesVars>(*primal_fluxes))...,
380  expanded_fluxes_args...,
381  get<AuxiliaryVars>(*auxiliary_vars)...);
382  },
383  fluxes_args);
384  }
385 
386  // Populate the mortar data on this element's side of the boundary so it's
387  // ready to be sent to neighbors.
388  for (const auto& direction : [&element]() noexcept -> decltype(auto) {
389  if constexpr (DataIsZero) {
390  // Skipping internal boundaries for zero data because they won't
391  // contribute boundary corrections anyway.
392  return element.external_boundaries();
393  } else {
394  return boost::join(element.internal_boundaries(),
395  element.external_boundaries());
396  };
397  }()) {
398  if (not directions_predicate(direction)) {
399  continue;
400  }
401  const bool is_internal = element.neighbors().contains(direction);
402  const auto face_mesh = mesh.slice_away(direction.dimension());
403  const size_t face_num_points = face_mesh.number_of_grid_points();
404  const auto& face_normal = is_internal
405  ? internal_face_normals.at(direction)
406  : external_face_normals.at(direction);
407  const auto& face_normal_magnitude =
408  is_internal ? internal_face_normal_magnitudes.at(direction)
409  : external_face_normal_magnitudes.at(direction);
410  const auto& fluxes_args_on_face =
411  is_internal ? fluxes_args_on_internal_faces.at(direction)
412  : fluxes_args_on_external_faces.at(direction);
413  const size_t slice_index = index_to_slice_at(mesh.extents(), direction);
414  Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalFluxesVars..., AuxiliaryFluxesVars...>>
415  fluxes_on_face{face_num_points};
416  Variables<tmpl::list<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<AuxiliaryFields>...>>
417  n_dot_aux_fluxes{face_num_points};
418  BoundaryData<tmpl::list<PrimalMortarVars...>,
419  tmpl::list<PrimalMortarFluxes...>>
420  boundary_data{face_num_points};
421  if constexpr (DataIsZero) {
422  // Just setting all boundary field data to zero. Variable-independent
423  // data such as the element size will be set below.
424  boundary_data.field_data.initialize(face_num_points, 0.);
425  } else {
426  // Compute F_u(n.F_v)
427  fluxes_on_face.assign_subset(
428  data_on_slice(*auxiliary_fluxes, mesh.extents(),
429  direction.dimension(), slice_index));
430  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(normal_dot_flux(
431  make_not_null(
432  &get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<AuxiliaryFields>>(n_dot_aux_fluxes)),
433  face_normal, get<AuxiliaryFluxesVars>(fluxes_on_face)));
434  std::apply(
435  [&boundary_data, &n_dot_aux_fluxes](
436  const auto&... expanded_fluxes_args_on_face) noexcept {
437  FluxesComputer::apply(
438  make_not_null(&get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
439  boundary_data.field_data))...,
440  expanded_fluxes_args_on_face...,
441  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<AuxiliaryFields>>(
442  n_dot_aux_fluxes)...);
443  },
444  fluxes_args_on_face);
445  // Compute n.F_u
446  //
447  // For the internal penalty flux we can already slice the n.F_u to the
448  // faces at this point, before the boundary corrections have been added
449  // to the auxiliary variables. The reason is essentially that the
450  // internal penalty flux uses average(grad(u)) in place of average(v),
451  // i.e. the raw primal field derivatives without boundary corrections.
452  fluxes_on_face.assign_subset(
453  data_on_slice(*primal_fluxes, mesh.extents(), direction.dimension(),
454  slice_index));
455  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(normal_dot_flux(
456  make_not_null(&get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarVars>>(
457  boundary_data.field_data)),
458  face_normal, get<PrimalFluxesVars>(fluxes_on_face)));
459  // Compute n.F_u(n.F_v) for jump term, re-using the memory buffer from
460  // above
461  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(normal_dot_flux(
462  make_not_null(&get<Tags::NormalDotFluxForJump<PrimalMortarVars>>(
463  boundary_data.field_data)),
464  face_normal,
465  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
466  boundary_data.field_data)));
467  }
468 
469  // Collect the remaining data that's needed on both sides of the boundary
470  // These are actually constant throughout the solve, so a performance
471  // optimization could be to store them in the DataBox.
472  get<Tags::PerpendicularNumPoints>(boundary_data.extra_data) =
473  mesh.extents(direction.dimension());
474  get(get<Tags::ElementSize>(boundary_data.field_data)) =
475  2. / get(face_normal_magnitude);
476 
477  if (is_internal) {
478  if constexpr (not DataIsZero) {
479  // Project boundary data on internal faces to mortars
480  for (const auto& neighbor_id : element.neighbors().at(direction)) {
481  const ::dg::MortarId<Dim> mortar_id{direction, neighbor_id};
482  const auto& mortar_mesh = all_mortar_meshes.at(mortar_id);
483  const auto& mortar_size = all_mortar_sizes.at(mortar_id);
484  auto projected_boundary_data =
485  Spectral::needs_projection(face_mesh, mortar_mesh, mortar_size)
486  ? boundary_data.project_to_mortar(face_mesh, mortar_mesh,
487  mortar_size)
488  : std::move(boundary_data);
489  (*all_mortar_data)[mortar_id].local_insert(
490  temporal_id, std::move(projected_boundary_data));
491  }
492  }
493  } else {
494  // No need to do projections on external boundaries
495  const ::dg::MortarId<Dim> mortar_id{
496  direction, ElementId<Dim>::external_boundary_id()};
497  (*all_mortar_data)[mortar_id].local_insert(temporal_id, boundary_data);
498 
499  // -------------------------
500  // Apply boundary conditions
501  // -------------------------
502  //
503  // To apply boundary conditions we fill the boundary data with
504  // "exterior" or "ghost" data and set it as remote mortar data, so
505  // external boundaries behave just like internal boundaries when
506  // applying boundary corrections.
507  //
508  // The `apply_boundary_conditions` invocable is expected to impose
509  // boundary conditions by modifying the fields and fluxes that are
510  // passed by reference. Dirichlet-type boundary conditions are imposed
511  // by modifying the fields, and Neumann-type boundary conditions are
512  // imposed by modifying the interior n.F_u. Note that all data passed to
513  // the boundary conditions is taken from the "interior" side of the
514  // boundary, i.e. with a normal vector that points _out_ of the
515  // computational domain.
516  auto dirichlet_vars = data_on_slice(primal_vars, mesh.extents(),
517  direction.dimension(), slice_index);
518  apply_boundary_condition(
519  direction, make_not_null(&get<PrimalVars>(dirichlet_vars))...,
520  make_not_null(&get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarVars>>(
521  boundary_data.field_data))...);
522 
523  // The n.F_u (Neumann-type conditions) are done, but we have to compute
524  // the n.F_v (Dirichlet-type conditions) from the Dirichlet fields. We
525  // re-use the memory buffer from above.
526  std::apply(
527  [&fluxes_on_face, &dirichlet_vars](
528  const auto&... expanded_fluxes_args_on_face) noexcept {
529  FluxesComputer::apply(
530  make_not_null(&get<AuxiliaryFluxesVars>(fluxes_on_face))...,
531  expanded_fluxes_args_on_face...,
532  get<PrimalVars>(dirichlet_vars)...);
533  },
534  fluxes_args_on_face);
535  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(normal_dot_flux(
536  make_not_null(
537  &get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<AuxiliaryFields>>(n_dot_aux_fluxes)),
538  face_normal, get<AuxiliaryFluxesVars>(fluxes_on_face)));
539  std::apply(
540  [&boundary_data, &n_dot_aux_fluxes](
541  const auto&... expanded_fluxes_args_on_face) noexcept {
542  FluxesComputer::apply(
543  make_not_null(&get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
544  boundary_data.field_data))...,
545  expanded_fluxes_args_on_face...,
546  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<AuxiliaryFields>>(
547  n_dot_aux_fluxes)...);
548  },
549  fluxes_args_on_face);
550 
551  // Invert the sign of the fluxes to account for the inverted normal on
552  // exterior faces. Also multiply by 2 and add the interior fluxes to
553  // impose the boundary conditions on the _average_ instead of just
554  // setting the fields on the exterior.
555  const auto invert_sign_and_impose_on_average =
556  [](const auto exterior_n_dot_flux,
557  const auto& interior_n_dot_flux) {
558  for (size_t i = 0; i < interior_n_dot_flux.size(); ++i) {
559  (*exterior_n_dot_flux)[i] *= -2.;
560  (*exterior_n_dot_flux)[i] += interior_n_dot_flux[i];
561  }
562  };
563  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(invert_sign_and_impose_on_average(
564  make_not_null(&get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarVars>>(
565  boundary_data.field_data)),
566  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarVars>>(
567  all_mortar_data->at(mortar_id)
568  .local_data(temporal_id)
569  .field_data)));
570  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(invert_sign_and_impose_on_average(
571  make_not_null(&get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
572  boundary_data.field_data)),
573  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
574  all_mortar_data->at(mortar_id)
575  .local_data(temporal_id)
576  .field_data)));
577 
578  // Compute n.F_u(n.F_v) for jump term
579  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(normal_dot_flux(
580  make_not_null(&get<Tags::NormalDotFluxForJump<PrimalMortarVars>>(
581  boundary_data.field_data)),
582  face_normal,
583  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
584  boundary_data.field_data)));
585  const auto invert_sign = [](const auto exterior_n_dot_flux) {
586  for (size_t i = 0; i < exterior_n_dot_flux->size(); ++i) {
587  (*exterior_n_dot_flux)[i] *= -1.;
588  }
589  };
590  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(invert_sign(
591  make_not_null(&get<Tags::NormalDotFluxForJump<PrimalMortarVars>>(
592  boundary_data.field_data))));
593 
594  // Store the exterior boundary data on the mortar
595  all_mortar_data->at(mortar_id).remote_insert(temporal_id,
596  std::move(boundary_data));
597  } // if (is_internal)
598  } // loop directions
599  }

603  template <bool DataIsZero, typename... OperatorTags, typename... PrimalVars,
604  typename... PrimalFluxesVars, typename... PrimalMortarVars,
605  typename... PrimalMortarFluxes, typename TemporalId,
606  typename... FluxesArgs, typename... SourcesArgs,
607  typename DirectionsPredicate = AllDirections>
608  static void apply_operator(
609  const gsl::not_null<Variables<tmpl::list<OperatorTags...>>*>
610  operator_applied_to_vars,
611  const gsl::not_null<::dg::MortarMap<
612  Dim, MortarData<TemporalId, tmpl::list<PrimalMortarVars...>,
613  tmpl::list<PrimalMortarFluxes...>>>*>
614  all_mortar_data,
615  const Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalVars...>>& primal_vars,
616  // Taking the primal fluxes computed in the `prepare_mortar_data` function
617  // by const-ref here because other code might use them and so we don't
618  // want to modify them by adding boundary corrections. E.g. linearized
619  // sources use the nonlinear fields and fluxes as background fields.
620  const Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalFluxesVars...>>& primal_fluxes,
621  const Mesh<Dim>& mesh,
622  const InverseJacobian<DataVector, Dim, Frame::Logical, Frame::Inertial>&
623  inv_jacobian,
624  const Scalar<DataVector>& det_inv_jacobian,
625  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, Scalar<DataVector>>&
626  internal_face_normal_magnitudes,
627  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, Scalar<DataVector>>&
628  external_face_normal_magnitudes,
629  const ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, Mesh<Dim - 1>>& all_mortar_meshes,
630  const ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, ::dg::MortarSize<Dim - 1>>& all_mortar_sizes,
631  const double penalty_parameter, const bool massive,
632  const TemporalId& temporal_id,
633  const std::tuple<SourcesArgs...>& sources_args,
634  const DirectionsPredicate& directions_predicate =
635  AllDirections{}) noexcept {
636  static_assert(
637  sizeof...(PrimalVars) == sizeof...(PrimalFields) and
638  sizeof...(PrimalFluxesVars) == sizeof...(PrimalFluxes) and
639  sizeof...(PrimalMortarVars) == sizeof...(PrimalFields) and
640  sizeof...(PrimalMortarFluxes) == sizeof...(PrimalFluxes) and
641  sizeof...(OperatorTags) == sizeof...(PrimalFields),
642  "The number of variables must match the number of system fields.");
643  static_assert(
644  (std::is_same_v<typename PrimalVars::type,
645  typename PrimalFields::type> and
646  ...) and
647  (std::is_same_v<typename PrimalFluxesVars::type,
648  typename PrimalFluxes::type> and
649  ...) and
650  (std::is_same_v<typename PrimalMortarVars::type,
651  typename PrimalFields::type> and
652  ...) and
653  (std::is_same_v<typename PrimalMortarFluxes::type,
654  typename PrimalFluxes::type> and
655  ...) and
656  (std::is_same_v<typename OperatorTags::type,
657  typename PrimalFields::type> and
658  ...),
659  "The variables must have the same tensor types as the system fields.");
660 #ifdef SPECTRE_DEBUG
661  for (size_t d = 0; d < Dim; ++d) {
662  ASSERT(mesh.basis(d) == Spectral::Basis::Legendre and
663  mesh.quadrature(d) == Spectral::Quadrature::GaussLobatto,
664  "The elliptic DG operator is currently only implemented for "
665  "Legendre-Gauss-Lobatto grids. Found basis '"
666  << mesh.basis(d) << "' and quadrature '" << mesh.quadrature(d)
667  << "' in dimension " << d << ".");
668  }
669 #endif // SPECTRE_DEBUG
670 
671  // This function and the one above allocate various Variables to compute
672  // intermediate quantities. It could be a performance optimization to reduce
673  // the number of these allocations and/or move some of the memory buffers
674  // into the DataBox to keep them around permanently. The latter should be
675  // informed by profiling.
676 
677  // Add boundary corrections to the auxiliary variables _before_ computing
678  // the second derivative. This is called the "flux" formulation. It is
679  // equivalent to discretizing the system in first-order form, i.e. treating
680  // the primal and auxiliary variables on the same footing, and then taking a
681  // Schur complement of the operator. The Schur complement is possible
682  // because the auxiliary equations are essentially the definition of the
683  // auxiliary variables and can therefore always be solved for the auxiliary
684  // variables by just inverting the mass matrix. This Schur-complement
685  // formulation avoids inflating the DG operator with the DOFs of the
686  // auxiliary variables. In this form it is very similar to the "primal"
687  // formulation where we get rid of the auxiliary variables through a DG
688  // theorem and thus add the auxiliary boundary corrections _after_ computing
689  // the second derivative. This involves a slightly different lifting
690  // operation with differentiation matrices, which we avoid to implement for
691  // now by using the flux-formulation.
692  auto primal_fluxes_corrected = primal_fluxes;
693  for (const auto& [mortar_id, mortar_data] : *all_mortar_data) {
694  const auto& [direction, neighbor_id] = mortar_id;
695  const bool is_internal =
696  (neighbor_id != ElementId<Dim>::external_boundary_id());
697  if constexpr (DataIsZero) {
698  if (is_internal) {
699  continue;
700  }
701  }
702  if (not directions_predicate(direction)) {
703  continue;
704  }
705  const auto face_mesh = mesh.slice_away(direction.dimension());
706  const size_t slice_index = index_to_slice_at(mesh.extents(), direction);
707  const auto& local_data = mortar_data.local_data(temporal_id);
708  const auto& remote_data = mortar_data.remote_data(temporal_id);
709  const auto& face_normal_magnitude =
710  is_internal ? internal_face_normal_magnitudes.at(direction)
711  : external_face_normal_magnitudes.at(direction);
712  const auto& mortar_mesh =
713  is_internal ? all_mortar_meshes.at(mortar_id) : face_mesh;
714  const auto& mortar_size =
715  is_internal ? all_mortar_sizes.at(mortar_id) : full_mortar_size;
716 
717  // This is the _strong_ auxiliary boundary correction avg(n.F_v) - n.F_v
718  auto auxiliary_boundary_corrections_on_mortar =
719  Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalMortarFluxes...>>(
720  local_data.field_data.template extract_subset<
721  tmpl::list<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>...>>());
722  const auto add_remote_contribution = [](auto& lhs,
723  const auto& rhs) noexcept {
724  for (size_t i = 0; i < lhs.size(); ++i) {
725  lhs[i] += rhs[i];
726  }
727  };
728  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(add_remote_contribution(
729  get<PrimalMortarFluxes>(auxiliary_boundary_corrections_on_mortar),
730  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarFluxes>>(
731  remote_data.field_data)));
732  auxiliary_boundary_corrections_on_mortar *= -0.5;
733 
734  // Project from the mortar back down to the face if needed
735  auto auxiliary_boundary_corrections =
736  Spectral::needs_projection(face_mesh, mortar_mesh, mortar_size)
737  ? ::dg::project_from_mortar(
738  auxiliary_boundary_corrections_on_mortar, face_mesh,
739  mortar_mesh, mortar_size)
740  : std::move(auxiliary_boundary_corrections_on_mortar);
741 
742  // Lift the boundary correction to the volume, but still only provide the
743  // data only on the face because it is zero everywhere else. This is the
744  // "massless" lifting operation, i.e. it involves an inverse mass matrix.
745  // The mass matrix is diagonally approximated ("mass lumping") so it
746  // reduces to a division by quadrature weights.
747  ::dg::lift_flux(make_not_null(&auxiliary_boundary_corrections),
748  mesh.extents(direction.dimension()),
749  face_normal_magnitude);
750  // The `dg::lift_flux` function contains an extra minus sign
751  auxiliary_boundary_corrections *= -1.;
752 
753  // Add the boundary corrections to the auxiliary variables
754  add_slice_to_data(make_not_null(&primal_fluxes_corrected),
755  auxiliary_boundary_corrections, mesh.extents(),
756  direction.dimension(), slice_index);
757  } // apply auxiliary boundary corrections on all mortars
758 
759  // Compute the primal equation, i.e. the actual DG operator, by taking the
760  // second derivative: -div(F_u(v)) + S_u = f(x)
761  divergence(operator_applied_to_vars, primal_fluxes_corrected, mesh,
762  inv_jacobian);
763  // This is the sign flip that makes the operator _minus_ the Laplacian for a
764  // Poisson system
765  *operator_applied_to_vars *= -1.;
766  // Using the non-boundary-corrected primal fluxes to compute sources here
767  std::apply(
768  [&operator_applied_to_vars, &primal_vars,
769  &primal_fluxes](const auto&... expanded_sources_args) noexcept {
770  SourcesComputer::apply(
771  make_not_null(&get<OperatorTags>(*operator_applied_to_vars))...,
772  expanded_sources_args..., get<PrimalVars>(primal_vars)...,
773  get<PrimalFluxesVars>(primal_fluxes)...);
774  },
775  sources_args);
776 
777  // Add boundary corrections to primal equation
778  for (auto& [mortar_id, mortar_data] : *all_mortar_data) {
779  const auto& [direction, neighbor_id] = mortar_id;
780  const bool is_internal =
781  (neighbor_id != ElementId<Dim>::external_boundary_id());
782  if constexpr (DataIsZero) {
783  if (is_internal) {
784  continue;
785  }
786  }
787  const auto face_mesh = mesh.slice_away(direction.dimension());
788  const size_t slice_index = index_to_slice_at(mesh.extents(), direction);
789  const auto [local_data, remote_data] = mortar_data.extract();
790  const auto& face_normal_magnitude =
791  is_internal ? internal_face_normal_magnitudes.at(direction)
792  : external_face_normal_magnitudes.at(direction);
793  const auto& mortar_mesh =
794  is_internal ? all_mortar_meshes.at(mortar_id) : face_mesh;
795  const auto& mortar_size =
796  is_internal ? all_mortar_sizes.at(mortar_id) : full_mortar_size;
797 
798  // This is the _strong_ primal boundary correction avg(n.F_u) - penalty *
799  // jump(n.F_v) - n.F_u. Note that the "internal penalty" numerical flux
800  // (as opposed to the LLF flux) uses the raw field derivatives without
801  // boundary corrections in the average, which is why we can communicate
802  // the data so early together with the auxiliary boundary data. In this
803  // case the penalty needs to include a factor N_points^2 / h (see the
804  // `penalty` function).
805  const auto penalty = elliptic::dg::penalty(
806  min(get(get<Tags::ElementSize>(local_data.field_data)),
807  get(get<Tags::ElementSize>(remote_data.field_data))),
808  std::max(get<Tags::PerpendicularNumPoints>(local_data.extra_data),
809  get<Tags::PerpendicularNumPoints>(remote_data.extra_data)),
810  penalty_parameter);
811  // Start with the penalty term
812  auto primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar =
813  Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalMortarVars...>>(
814  local_data.field_data.template extract_subset<tmpl::list<
815  Tags::NormalDotFluxForJump<PrimalMortarVars>...>>());
816  const auto add_remote_jump_contribution = [](auto& lhs,
817  const auto& rhs) noexcept {
818  for (size_t i = 0; i < lhs.size(); ++i) {
819  lhs[i] -= rhs[i];
820  }
821  };
822  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(add_remote_jump_contribution(
823  get<PrimalMortarVars>(primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar),
824  get<Tags::NormalDotFluxForJump<PrimalMortarVars>>(
825  remote_data.field_data)));
826  primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar *= penalty;
827  const auto add_remote_avg_contribution = [](auto& lhs,
828  const auto& rhs) noexcept {
829  for (size_t i = 0; i < lhs.size(); ++i) {
830  lhs[i] += 0.5 * rhs[i];
831  }
832  };
833  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(add_remote_avg_contribution(
834  get<PrimalMortarVars>(primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar),
835  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarVars>>(local_data.field_data)));
836  EXPAND_PACK_LEFT_TO_RIGHT(add_remote_avg_contribution(
837  get<PrimalMortarVars>(primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar),
838  get<::Tags::NormalDotFlux<PrimalMortarVars>>(
839  remote_data.field_data)));
840  primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar *= -1.;
841 
842  // Project from the mortar back down to the face if needed, lift and add
843  // to operator. See auxiliary boundary corrections above for details.
844  auto primal_boundary_corrections =
845  Spectral::needs_projection(face_mesh, mortar_mesh, mortar_size)
846  ? ::dg::project_from_mortar(primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar,
847  face_mesh, mortar_mesh, mortar_size)
848  : std::move(primal_boundary_corrections_on_mortar);
849  ::dg::lift_flux(make_not_null(&primal_boundary_corrections),
850  mesh.extents(direction.dimension()),
851  face_normal_magnitude);
852  add_slice_to_data(operator_applied_to_vars, primal_boundary_corrections,
853  mesh.extents(), direction.dimension(), slice_index);
854  } // loop over all mortars
855 
856  // Apply DG mass matrix
857  if (massive) {
858  *operator_applied_to_vars /= get(det_inv_jacobian);
859  ::dg::apply_mass_matrix(operator_applied_to_vars, mesh);
860  }
861  }

863  template <typename... FixedSourcesTags, typename ApplyBoundaryCondition,
864  typename... FluxesArgs, typename... SourcesArgs,
865  bool LocalLinearized = Linearized,
866  // This function adds nothing to the fixed sources if the operator
867  // is linearized, so it shouldn't be used in that case
868  Requires<not LocalLinearized> = nullptr>
869  static void impose_inhomogeneous_boundary_conditions_on_source(
870  const gsl::not_null<Variables<tmpl::list<FixedSourcesTags...>>*>
871  fixed_sources,
872  const Element<Dim>& element, const Mesh<Dim>& mesh,
873  const InverseJacobian<DataVector, Dim, Frame::Logical, Frame::Inertial>&
874  inv_jacobian,
875  const Scalar<DataVector>& det_inv_jacobian,
876  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, tnsr::i<DataVector, Dim>>&
877  external_face_normals,
878  const std::unordered_map<Direction<Dim>, Scalar<DataVector>>&
879  external_face_normal_magnitudes,
880  const ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, Mesh<Dim - 1>>& all_mortar_meshes,
881  const ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, ::dg::MortarSize<Dim - 1>>& all_mortar_sizes,
882  const double penalty_parameter, const bool massive,
883  const ApplyBoundaryCondition& apply_boundary_condition,
884  const std::tuple<FluxesArgs...>& fluxes_args,
885  const std::tuple<SourcesArgs...>& sources_args,
886  const DirectionMap<Dim, std::tuple<FluxesArgs...>>&
887  fluxes_args_on_internal_faces,
888  const DirectionMap<Dim, std::tuple<FluxesArgs...>>&
889  fluxes_args_on_external_faces) noexcept {
890  // We just feed zero variables through the nonlinear operator to extract the
891  // constant contribution at external boundaries. Since the variables are
892  // zero the operator simplifies quite a lot. The simplification is probably
893  // not very important for performance because this function will only be
894  // called when solving a linear elliptic system and only once during
895  // initialization, but we specialize the operator for zero data nonetheless
896  // just so we can ignore internal boundaries. For internal boundaries we
897  // would unnecessarily have to copy mortar data around to emulate the
898  // communication step, so by just skipping internal boundaries we avoid
899  // that.
900  const size_t num_points = mesh.number_of_grid_points();
901  const Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalFields...>> zero_primal_vars{num_points,
902  0.};
903  Variables<tmpl::list<PrimalFluxes...>> primal_fluxes_buffer{num_points};
904  Variables<tmpl::list<AuxiliaryFields...>> unused_aux_vars_buffer{};
905  Variables<tmpl::list<AuxiliaryFluxes...>> unused_aux_fluxes_buffer{};
906  Variables<tmpl::list<FixedSourcesTags...>> operator_applied_to_zero_vars{
907  num_points};
908  // Set up data on mortars. We only need them at external boundaries.
909  ::dg::MortarMap<Dim, MortarData<size_t, tmpl::list<PrimalFields...>,
910  tmpl::list<PrimalFluxes...>>>
911  all_mortar_data{};
912  constexpr size_t temporal_id = std::numeric_limits<size_t>::max();
913  // Apply the operator to the zero variables, skipping internal boundaries
914  prepare_mortar_data<true>(
915  make_not_null(&unused_aux_vars_buffer),
916  make_not_null(&unused_aux_fluxes_buffer),
917  make_not_null(&primal_fluxes_buffer), make_not_null(&all_mortar_data),
918  zero_primal_vars, element, mesh, inv_jacobian, {},
919  external_face_normals, {}, external_face_normal_magnitudes,
920  all_mortar_meshes, all_mortar_sizes, temporal_id,
921  apply_boundary_condition, fluxes_args, sources_args,
922  fluxes_args_on_internal_faces, fluxes_args_on_external_faces);
923  apply_operator<true>(make_not_null(&operator_applied_to_zero_vars),
924  make_not_null(&all_mortar_data), zero_primal_vars,
925  primal_fluxes_buffer, mesh, inv_jacobian,
926  det_inv_jacobian, {}, external_face_normal_magnitudes,
927  all_mortar_meshes, all_mortar_sizes, penalty_parameter,
928  massive, temporal_id, sources_args);
929  // Impose the nonlinear (constant) boundary contribution as fixed sources on
930  // the RHS of the equations
931  *fixed_sources -= operator_applied_to_zero_vars;
932  }
933 };

965  * \brief For linear systems, impose inhomogeneous boundary conditions as
966  * contributions to the fixed sources (i.e. the RHS of the equations).
967  *
968  * This function exists because the DG operator must typically be linear, but
969  * even for linear elliptic equations we typically apply boundary conditions
970  * with a constant, and therefore nonlinear, contribution. Standard examples are
971  * inhomogeneous (i.e. non-zero) Dirichlet or Neumann boundary conditions. This
972  * nonlinear contribution can be added to the fixed sources, leaving only the
973  * linearized boundary conditions in the DG operator. For standard constant
974  * Dirichlet or Neumann boundary conditions the linearization is of course just
975  * zero.
976  *
977  * This function essentially feeds zero variables through the nonlinear operator
978  * and subtracts the result from the fixed sources: `b -= A(x=0)`.
979  */
